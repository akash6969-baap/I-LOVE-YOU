Two Poems
Page 01
Nature's Lullaby
The breeze hums soft through silver trees,
And mountain streams hum quiet tunes,
The stars above share gentle ease,
As night unfolds its silver moons.

The birds return to their old nests,
The sun dips low behind the hill,
The world grows calm and slowly rests —
The night at last is soft and still.
The Dreamer
I walk through halls of golden thought,
Where every dream is dearly bought,
I chase the clouds, I touch the sky,
I never stop to wonder why.

For dreamers know no walls of fear,
They see the distant far as near,
They paint their worlds in vivid hues —
And win the life they dare to choose.

Climate Crisis
Rising Temperatures: The Urgency of Climate Action
Climate change is no longer a distant threat — it is a present reality. Scientists worldwide report that global temperatures have risen by over 1.1°C since pre-industrial times. Glaciers are melting, sea levels are rising, and extreme weather events are becoming more frequent. World leaders are under pressure to cut carbon emissions and invest in renewable energy before the damage becomes irreversible.
Technology
Artificial Intelligence: Promise and Peril in the Modern Age
Artificial intelligence is transforming every field — from healthcare to education and transportation. AI-powered tools are helping doctors detect diseases earlier, making learning more personalised, and powering self-driving vehicles. Yet experts raise important questions about job displacement, privacy, and the ethical use of AI. The challenge for society is to harness AI's power while ensuring it remains safe and fair for all.

My Travel Diary
Rishikesh, Uttarakhand
Where the Himalayas meet the holy Ganga — a journey of a lifetime.

The morning we set off for Rishikesh, the air smelled of pine and possibility. Our car wound through the hills as mist hung low over the valleys, and I felt a quiet excitement I had never known before.

"Standing at the Lakshman Jhula bridge, watching the river roar below, I understood why people call this place the yoga capital of the world."

We spent our first evening at the Ganga aarti near Triveni Ghat. Hundreds of oil lamps floated on the dark water, flickering orange and gold. The chanting echoed off the mountains. Time seemed to stop.

The next day we tried white-water rafting — twelve kilometres of pure, screaming joy. Every wave was a surprise, every calm stretch was a chance to breathe. By the end, we were soaked, laughing, and completely alive.

Interview
Page 05
An Interview with My Grandmother — She turns 72 this year. She has seen India change across seven decades. This is her story, in her words.
Q — Interviewer
Dadi, what was your childhood like? How different was the world back then?
A — Dadi
Oh, life was so simple. We had no television, no phones — only each other. We played in open fields, we ate together as a family every single meal, and we found joy in the smallest things. The world was slower, kinder somehow.
Q — Interviewer
What is the one thing you wish today's generation understood better?
A — Dadi
Patience. Everything today is instant — food, messages, entertainment. But the best things in life — respect, wisdom, deep love — they only come with time. Never be in a hurry to grow up. Each moment is a gift.

Inspirational Story
Page 06
"The man who moves a mountain begins by carrying away small stones."
— Confucius

Dashrath Manjhi — The Mountain Man


Dashrath Manjhi was a poor labourer from Gehlaur village in Bihar. When his wife Falguni Devi fell ill and died because the nearest hospital was 70 km away — blocked by a mountain — he made a decision that the world called madness.


Armed with only a hammer and chisel, he spent 22 years carving a road 110 metres long through a rocky mountain — completely alone. When the road was complete, the distance between two villages shrank from 55 km to just 15 km.


Dashrath Manjhi never sought fame. He acted simply because someone had to. His story teaches us that ordinary people, driven by extraordinary purpose, can reshape the world.

✦ Lesson: You don't need tools or power to change the world. You only need will, patience, and love.

The Best Day of My Life
Page 08

Some days are so full of joy that they never leave you. For me, that day was the day our school won the inter-school debate competition — and I had been part of the team.

7:00 AM
The Morning of Butterflies
I woke up before my alarm. My notes were everywhere. My heart was racing. But something felt different — electric.
11:00 AM
The Stage, The Lights, The Moment
When my name was called, I walked to the podium. The hall went quiet. I took one deep breath and began to speak.
2:30 PM
The Announcement
"And the winner of the inter-school debate is..." — our school's name filled the auditorium. We jumped, we screamed, we cried.
Evening
The Celebration
We celebrated with ice cream and long conversations. Ma hugged me at the door. That hug made the whole day complete.

That day taught me that the moments we work hardest for become the ones we treasure most. I carry it with me everywhere I go.
